This matter is before the undersigned Chair of the North Carolina State Bar's Grievance
Committee pursuant to 27 NCAC IB §.OII8(g). Deputy Counsel A. Root Edmonson represents
the North Carolina State Bar (the "State Bar"). Christopher Todd Rhodes is unrepresented.
Based upon the information Rhodes provided and with his consent, and the consent of deputy
counsel, the undersigned makes the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1. The State Bar is a body duly organized under the laws ofNOlih Carolina and is the
proper party to consent to this order under the authority granted it in Chapter 84 of the General
Statutes of North Carolina, and the Rules and Regulations ofthe North Carolina State Bar
(Chapter I of Title 27 of the North Carolina Administrative Code ("NCAC").

2. Rhodes was admitted to the NOlih Carolina State Bar on September 18, 1992, and is,
and was at all times referred to herein, an attorney at law licensed to practice in North Carolina,
subject to the rules, regulations and Rules of Professional Conduct of the NOlih Carolina State
Bar and the laws of the State of North Carolina.
3. During or prior to the relevant periods referred to herein, Rhodes was engaged in the
practice oflaw in the State of North Carolina and maintained a law olTice in Greensboro,
Guilford County, North Carolina.
4. Rhodes presently suffers from a mental condition that significantly impairs his
professional judgment, performance or competence as an attorney.
5. Dr. Phuong H. Nguyen-Antonio, D.O., a Winston-Salem psychiatrist, diagnosed
Rhodes as suffering from Major Depressive Disorder. It is Dr. Nguyen-Antonio's opinion that
Rhodes' condition impairs his professional judgment and performance as an attorney.
6. Rhodes consents to being transferred to disability inactive status.

Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the undersigned makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1. There is clear, cogent and convincing evidence that Rhodes is disabled within the
meaning of27 NCAC IB § .0103 (19).
2. Rhodes should be transfelTed to disability inactive status with the State Bar.
3. Rhodes has voluntarily consented to being transferred to disability inactive status
pursuant to 27 NCAC IB § .0118 (g).
4. The above-referenced grievances, and any others against Rhodes, shall be stayed
during the period of disability.
TI-IEREFORE, it is hereby ORDERED:
1. Christopher Todd Rhodes is transferred to disability inactive status.
2. Rhodes shall not practice law in North Carolina until he is no longer disabled and has
been returned to active status as a member of the North Carolina State Bar pursuant to the
procedures set out in 27 NCAC IB, §.0125(c).
3. The pending grievances against Rhodes shall be stayed during the period of disability.
