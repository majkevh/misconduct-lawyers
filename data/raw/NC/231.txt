This matter coming on to be heard and being heard before the Council of the North
Carolina State Bar on July 9, 1993 pursuant to Section 17 of Article IX of the Rules of
Discipline and Disbarment upon the tender of surrender of license of Karl D. Leathers sworn
to and subscribed on June 11, 1993 and filed in the offices of the North Carolina State Bar on
June 14, 1993.
BASED UPON the affidavit, the Council finds that Karl D. Leathers is guilty of
trafficking by transportation, possession, sale, and delivery of cocaine in violation of N.C.
Gen. Stat. Sec. 84-28(b)(2) and Rule 1.2(B) of the Rules of Professional Conduct.
WHEREFORE, upon Motion being duly made and seconded, the Council enters the
following ORDER:
1.	

That the tender of surrender of license of Karl D. Leathers be accepted.

2.	

That Karl D. Leathers be and hereby is DISBARRED from the practice of
law in North Carolina.

3.	

That the costs ofthis action be taxed against Karl D. Leathers.

DONE AND ORDERED this the 9th day of July, 1993.

The undersigned, Karl D. Leathers, being first duly sworn,
deposes and says:
1.	

I desire to resign from the North Carolina State Bar and
hereby tender my license to practice law in the State of
North Carolina pursuant to Section 17 of the Rules and
Regulations of the North Carolina State Bar.

2.	

My resignation is tendered freely and voluntarily, is not
the result or coercion or duress, and I am fully aware of
the implications of submitting this resignation.

3.	

I am aware that there is presently pending an
investigation regarding allegations that I am guilty of
misconduct, to wit:
trafficking by transportation,
possession, sale, and delivery of cocaine in violation of
North Carolina General Section 90-95.

4.	

The undersigned acknowledges that the material facts by
which the grievance is predicated are true.

5.	

This resignation is being submitted because I know that
if charges were predicated upon the misconduct under
investigation, I could not successfully defend against
them.
