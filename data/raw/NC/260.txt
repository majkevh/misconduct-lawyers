TillS MATTER came on to be heard and was heard by the Secretary of the N. C.
State Bar pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code Chapter 1, Subchapter B, ยง.OI25(b) of the
N.C. State Bar Discipline & Disability Rules, upon receipt of the petition of A. Stanley
Mitchell dated July 29,1998 seeking reinstatement of his license to practice law in North
Carolina. Based upon a review of the records of the N.C. State Bar and the pleadings
filed herein, the Secretary makes the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1. Petitioner, A. Stanley Mitchell (hereafter, Mitchell), was suspended from the
practice oflaw in North Carolina for a period of three years, by order of the Disciplinary
Hearing Commission dated June 30, 1995.

2, The order suspending Mitchell's law license became effective on Aug. 24.
1995.
3. Mitchell has paid his mandatory N.C. State Bar dues for all years prior to
1998 and has paid the costs assessed against him in connection with his disciplinary case.
4, Mitchell has complied with the minimum continuing legal education
requirements for the two calendar years immediately preceding his suspension and has
paid all attendee fees and late charges assessed by the Board of Continuing Legal
Education.
5, The Client Security Fund has not disbursed any sums as the result of
Mitchell's misconduct.
6. Mitchell has not yet paid his 1998 mandatory N,C. State Bar dues.

Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the Secretary makes the following:
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1. The Petitioner, A. Stanley Mitchell, has satisfied the requirements set out in 27
N.C. Admin. Code Chapter 1, Subchapter B, ยง .OI25(b) of the Discipline & Disability
Rules of the N.C. State Bar.
2. Mitchell must tender his 1998 mandatory N.C. State Bar dues before being
reinstated to the active practice oflaw in North Carolina.
THEREFORE, it is hereby ORDERED:
I. A. Stanley Mitchell is hereby reinstated to the practice of law in North
Carolina as of the date upon which his 1998 mandatory N.C. State Bar dues in the
amount of$175 are received by the N.C. State Bar.
