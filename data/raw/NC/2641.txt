This matter came before the undersigned Chair of The Disciplinary Hearing Commission
pursuant to 27 N.C.A.C. 1B §.0121(d) upon an affidavit of surrender oflicense executed by Edna
Ruffin Walker on September 11,2017 and filed in the offices of the North Carolina State Bar on
September 13,2017.
Based upon the pleadings and the record, the undersigned makes the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1.
Plaintiff, the North Carolina State Bar (hereafter "State Bar"), is a body duly
organized under the laws of North Carolina and is the proper party to bring this proceeding
under the authority granted it in Chapter 84 of the General Statutes of North Carolina, and the
Rules and Regulations of the North Carolina State Bar promulgated thereunder. .
2.
Defendant, Edna Ruffin Walker, was licensed to practice law in North Carolina in
1996, and during all relevant periods herein she was an Attorney at Law subject to the rules,
regulations, and Rules of Professional Conduct of the North Carolina State Bar and the laws of
the State of North Carolina.
3.
On April 18, 2017, the North Carolina State Bar filed a formal complaint against
Defendant alleging that she willfully failed to timely file federal and state income tax returns for
the years 2008 and 2012 through 2014, failed to pay federal and state income taxes due for the
years 2008 through 2014, willfully failed to report FUTA and taxes withheld on behalf of her
employees, failed to pay the federal FUTA taxes owed to the IRS, failed to pay over to the IRS
the funds withheld from the paychecks of her employees as required by 26 U.S.C. §7202 and
engaged in technical violations of the trust accounting Rules, thereby violating the Rules of
Professional Conduct.
4.
This conduct as set forth in the complaint constitutes violations of Rule 8.4(b) and
(c), Rule 1.I5-2(a), (g) and (q), and Rule I.1 5-3(e) of the Rules of Professional Conduct and as
such constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. § 84-28(b)(2).

5.
Defendant has indicated her consent to disbannent by filing an affidavit of
surrender with the Disciplinary Hearing Commission. The affidavit meets all requirements set
forth in 27 N.C.A.C. IB §.0121(a)(I)-(4) and (d).
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the undersigned makes the following
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1.
27 N.C.A.C. I B §.0108(a) provides ~hat the Chair of the Disciplinary Hearing
Commission has the power and duty to enter orders disbarring members by consent.
2.
27 N.C.A.C. IB § .0121(d) provides that the Chair of the DHC witl accept the
surrender and issue an order of disbannent upon determination that an affidavit of surrender filed
pursuant to this subsection meets the requirements set out in 27 N.C.A.C. IB § .0121(a)(I), (2),
(3), (4).

3.
Defendant's affidavit meets all requirements set forth in 27 N.C.A.C. IB
§.0121(a)(I)-(4) and (d) and the facts upon which the affidavit is predicated warrant Defendant's
disbannent.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law, the undersigned
Chair of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission enters the following
ORDER
I.

The surrender of the license of Edna Ruffin Walker is hereby accepted.

2.
Edna Ruffin Walker is DISBARRED from the practice of law in North Carolina
effective upon the entry of this order with the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar.
3.
Edna Ruffin Walker shall comply with the provisions of27 N.C.A.C. IB
§ ,0121(c) and § .0128 of the State Bar Discipline and Disability Rules and shall surrender her
license and permanent membership card to the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar along
with the affidavit required by 27 N.C.A.C. 1B § .0128 of the State Bar Discipline and Disability
Rules.
4.

The costs and administrative fees of this action are taxed against Defendant.
