THIS MATTER is before the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar pursuant
to a Petition to Stay Suspension filed by Shonna R. Alexander ("Alexander") on 5
October 2009.
Based upon the petition and a review of the records of the North Carolina State
Bar, the Secretary makes the following;
FINDINGS OF FACT
I.
On 4 April 2008, a hearing committee of the Disciplinary Hearing
Commission ("DHC") entered Findings of Fact, Conclusions of Law, and Order of
Discipline ("Order of Discipline") in 07 DHC 10. The Order of Discipline suspended
Alexander from the practice of law for three years but pennitted Alexander, after one
year of active sllspension and upon compliance with certain conditions, to apply for a
stay of the remainder of the suspension.
2. On 5 October 2009, Alexander filed a Petition to Stay Suspension showing that
she had satisfied the conditions set forth in the Order of Discipline and requesting that the
remainder of the period ofsllspension imposed by the DI-IC be stayed.
3. Counsel for the North Carolina State Bar did not file an objection to
Alexander's petition.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the Secretary makes the following
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
I. The Petitioner, Shonna R. Alexander, has satisfied the requirements set out in
the Order of Discipline and the remainder ofthe suspension shall therefore be stayed.
