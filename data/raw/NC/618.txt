CONSENT ORDER
OF
DISBARMENT

_._-_._-THIS MATTER came on to be heard and was heard by the undersigned Judge of the
Superior Court of Wake County. Petitioner, the North Carolina State Bar, was represented by
Margaret T. Cloutier. Respondent, Christine C. Gates, was represented by Christopher G.
Chagaris.
Based upon the Affidavit of Surrender of Law License submitted to the court by Christine
C. Gates, the court makes the following:

FINDINGS OF FACT
I. Christine C. Gates operated a law office in Charlotte, North Carolina.
2. Gates combined and conspired with Mortgage Fraud Cell Nos. I, 2, 4 and 5 as alleged
in the Bill ofInformation filed in Case No. 3:09-cr-1 OO-FDW pending in the United States
District Court for the Western District of North Carolina.

3. Gates has entered a plea of guilty in 3:09-cr-l OO-FDW to the following criminal
offenses: (a) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell Nos. I, 2, 4 and 5 to
commit offenses against the United States, including making a false statement in relation to a
loan, and engaging in mai.1 fraud, wire fraud and bank fraud in violation of 18 U.S.c. 1014, 1341,
1343 and 1344; and (b) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell Nos. 1,
2,4 and 5 to launder monetary proceeds of fraudulent real estate transactions in violation of 18
U.S.c. 1956(h).
4. Gates is in fact guilty of these charges.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the couli makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1. By (a) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell Nos. 1,2,4 and
5 to commit offenses against the United States, including making a false statement in relation to a

loan, and engaging in mail fraud, wire fraud and bank fraud in violation of 18 U.S.c. 1014, 1341,
1343 and 1344; and (b) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell Nos. I,
2,4 and 5 to launder monetary proceeds of fraudulent real estate transactions in violation of 18
U.S.c. 1956(h), Gates violated Rules 8A(b) and (c) of the North Carolina Revised Rules of
Professional Conduct.
2. Gates' misconduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat.
Section 84-28(b)(2).

IT IS NOW, THEREFORE, ORDERED:
1. Christine C. Gates is DISBARRED from the practice of law in North Carolina.
2. Christine C. Gates shall comply with the provisions of Subchapter B, Section .0124 of
the Rules and Regulations of the North Carolina State Bar and shall surrender her license and
ptrmanentmembership card to the Secretary of the North Caroiina State Bar along with the
affidavit required by Subchapter B, Section .0124.
3. The costs of this action are taxed against Christine C. Gates.
