This matter is before the undersigned Chair of the Grievance Committee of the North
Carolina State Bar pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code 1B §.0118(g). Katherine E. Jean
represented the NOlth Carolina State Bar. Alan M. Schneider represented Reid G. Brown.
Based upon the evidence presented and with the consent of the member and of the Office of
Counsel, the undersigned makes the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1.

The NOlth Carolina State Bar is a body duly organized under the laws of North Carolina
and is the proper party to consent to this proceeding under the authority granted it in
Chapter 84 of the General Statutes of North Carolina, and the Rules and Regulations of
the NOlth Carolina State Bar promulgated thereunder.

2.

Reid G. Brown ("Brown") was admitted to the North Carolina State Bar on May 21, 1976
and is an Attorney at Law licensed to practice in North Carolina, subject to the rules,
regulations, and Rules of Professional Conduct of the North Carolina State Bar and the
laws of the State of North Carolina.

3.

Brown is actively engaged in the practice of law in the State of NOlth Carolina and
maintains a law office in Waynesville, NOlth Carolina.

4.

Brown is the respondent in grievance file numbers llG0401 and IIG0569, currently
pending before the Grievance Committee.

5.

Brown suffers from numerous medical conditions including but not limited to heart
disease, diabetes, neuropathy and arthritis. These conditions cause chronic pain, sleep
deprivation, cognitive impailment, anxiety and depression which significantly impair
Brown's professional judgment, perfOlmance, or competence as an attorney.

6.

Brown consents to being transferred to disability inactive status.

BASED UPON the foregoing Findings of Fact, the undersigned makes the following:
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1.

There is clear, cogent and convincing evidence that Brown is disabled within the meaning
of27 N.C. Admin. Code 1B §.0103(l9).

2.

Brown should be transferred to disability inactive status with the State Bar.

3.

Pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code IB §.0118(g), the proper parties have consented to
Brown being transferred to disability inactive status.

4.

The pending grievances should be stayed during the period of disability.

THEREfORE, it is hereby ORDERED:
1. Reid G. Brown is transferred to disability inactive status.
2. Brown shall not practice law in North Carolina until he is no longer disabled and has
been retumed to active status as a member of the North Carolina State Bar pursuant to the
procedures set out in 27 N.C. Admin. Code lB §.0125(c).
3. The pending grievances against Brown are hereby stayed during the period of disability
until such time as Brown retums to active status. The State Bar may continue to
investigate allegations of misconduct and preserve evidence as necessary pursuant to 27
N.C. Admin. Code lB §.0118(f).
