THIS CAUSE came on for consideration before the undersigned Chair of the
Disciplinary Hearing Commission pursuant to 27 N,CAC. 1 B § .0115(d).
Plaintiff, the North Carolina State Bar, is represented by Katherine E. Jean.
Defendant, Alexander L. Lapinski, is represented by Locke T. Clifford. Based upon
the Plea Agreement and the Factual Basis for a Guilty Plea contained In the record
and based upon the consent of the parties, tha undersigned mal<es the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1. Defendant was licensed to practice law in North Carolina In 2003.
2. Defendant's address of record with the North Carolina State Bar is
1906 E. NC Highway 54, Ste. 200-F, Durham, North Carolina 27713.
3. On Friday, March 16,2012, Defendanttendered a plea of guilty in
the United States District Court for the Middle District of North Carolina, in file
no. 1: 12CR86-1. to one count of unlawful proCLITement of CITizenship or
naturalization under 18 U.S.C. §1425 by aiding and abetting his client in
seeking United States citizenship under a false name, a felony.
4. The North Carolina State Bar will file a formal complaint against
Defendant alleging he has violated the Rules of Professional Conduct based
upon his felony conviction and upon the conduct on which tha conviction was
based.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact the Llndersigned makes the
following

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1. The crime of which Defendant was convicted Is a criminal offense
showing professional unfitness as defined in 27 N.C.A.C. lB § .0103 (17).
2. 27 N.C.A.C. 1B § .0115 provides that the Chair of the Disciplinary
Hearing Commission may enter an order suspending a member from
practicing law pending disposition of disciplinary proceedings besed upon
conviction of such crime.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law,
the undersigned Chair of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission enters the
following
ORDER
1. The license of Alexander L. Lapinsld to practice law In North
Carolina is hereby SUSPENDED until the conclusion of all disciplinary
proceedings before the Disciplinary Hearing Commission relating to
Defendant's felony conviction In the United States District Court for the Middle
District of North Carolina and relating to the conduct underlying such
conviction.
2. lapinski will comply with all requirements of 27 N.C.A.C. 1B § .0124
of the North CarOlina State Bar DiSCipline & Disability Rules for winding down
his law practice.
