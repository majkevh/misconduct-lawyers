This matter is before the undersigned Chair of the Grievance Committee of the North
Carolina State Bar pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code 1B § .0118(b). Brian P.D. Oten
represented the North Carolina State Bar ("State Bar"). Kevin L. Byrd was represented by Ollie
R Taylor. Based upon the information presented and with the consent of the member and of the
Office of Counsel, the undersigned makes the following
FINDINGS OF FACT
I.
The North Carolina State Bar ("State Bar") is a body dilly organized under the
laws of North Carolina and is the proper party to bring this proceeding under the authority
granted it in Chapter 84 of the General Statutes of North Carolina, and the rules and regulations
of the North Carolina State Bar promulgated thereunder.
2.
Kevin L. Byrd ("Byrd"), was admitted to the North Carolina State Bar on 24
March 1984 and is, and was at all times referred to herein, an attorney at law licensed to practice
in North Carolina, subject to the rilles, regulations and Rules of Professional Conduct of the
North Carolina State Bar and the laws of the State of North Carolina.
3.
During the times relevant herein, Byrd was engaged in the practice of law in the
State of North Carolina and maintained a law office in Cary, Wake County, North Carolina.
4.
Byrd currently suffers from bipolar disorder and depression which significantly
impairs his professional performance, judgment, and competence as an attorney.
5.

Byrd voluntarily consents to being transferred to disability inactive status.

6.

The State Bar consents to Byrd's transfer to disability inactive status.

Based on the foregoing Findings of Fact, the undersigned makes the following
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
I.
There is clear, cogent and convincing evidence that Byrd is disabled within the
meaning of27 N.C. Admin. Code 1B § .0103(19).
2.

Byrd should be transferred to disability inactive status with the State Bar.

THEREFORE, it is hereby ORDERED:
1.

Kevin L. Byrd is transferred to disability inactive status.

2.
Byrd shall not practice law in North Carolina until he is no longer disabled and
has been returned to active status as a member of the North Carolina State Bar pursuant to the
procedures set out in 27 N.C. Admin. Code lB § .0125(c).
3.
Any pending or future grievances against Byrd are hereby stayed during the
period of disability until such time as Byrd returns to active status. The State Bar may continue
to investigate allegations of misconduct and preserve evidence as necessary pursuant to 27 N.C.
Admin. Code 1B § .0118(e).
