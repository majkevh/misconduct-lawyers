THIS MATTER came before the undersigned Judge of the Superior Court of
Wake County upon the tender of surrender of her law license by Marsha B. Stone.
Based upon the Affidavit of Surrender of Law License submitted to the Court by
Respondent, Marsha B. Stone (hereinafter "Stone"), the Court makes the following:

FINDINGS OF FACT
1.
Stone was admitted to the North Carolina State Bar in 2004 and is, and
was at all times referred to herein, an attorney at law licensed to practice in North
Carolina, subject to the rules, regulations, and Rilles of Professional Conduct ofthe North
Carolina State Bar and the laws of the State of North Carolina.
2.
During all of the periods relevant hereto, Stone was engaged in the
praclice oflaw in Raleigh, North Carolina.
3.
Stone is aware that there is presently pending an investigation regarding
allegations that she is guilty of misconduct by misappropriating approximately
$23,780.00 from her attorney trust account entrusted to her in a fiduciary capacity and
utilized those funds for her own benefit without authorization from the beneficial owners
of the funds.
4.
Stone desires to resign her license to practice law and has tendered her

license to practice law in North Carolina.

5.
Stone's resignation is freely and voluntarily rendered. It is not the result

of coercion or duress. Stone is fully aware of the implication of submitting her

resignation.


6.
Stone acknowledges that the material facts upon which the investigation is
predicated are true.
7.
Stone has submitted her resignation because she knows that if disciplinary
charges were predicated upon the misconduct under investigation, she could not
successfully defend against them.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the court makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1.
By misappropriating client funds from her trust account, Stone engaged in
conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation in violation of Rule
8.4(c) ofthe Revised Rules ofProfessional Conduct.
2.
Stone's misconduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C.

Gen. Stat. § 84-28(b)(2).

3.
The courts of this State have the inherent authority to take disciplinary

action against attorneys licensed to practice law in North Carolina.

4.
This Court has jurisdiction over the person of Marsha B. Stone and of the
subject matter of this proceeding.
5.

Stone has engaged in professional misconduct warranting disbarment.

IT IS NOW, THEREFORE, ORDERED:
1.

Marsha B. Stone is DISBARRED from the practice oflaw in North


Carolina.

2.
Stone shall comply with the provisions of27 N.C. Admin. Code, Chapter
1, Subchapter B, Section .0124 of the Rules and Regulations of the North Carolina State
Bar and shall surrender her license and permanent membership card to the Secretary of
the North Carolina State Bar along with the affidavit required by § .0124. Stone shall
have thirty (30) days from the date of the entry of this order to complete the requirements
of § .0124.
3.
Stone shall not petition for reinstatement of her law license until the

expiration of at least five (5) years from the date of this order.

4.
Stone's law license shall not be reinstated until she has demonstrated to
the satisfaction of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission and the Council of the North

Carolina State Bar that she has complied with 27 N.C. Admin. Code Chapter 1,
Subchapter B, Section .0125.

5.

The costs of this action are taxed against Stone.
