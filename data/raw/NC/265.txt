ORDER OF DISBARMENT

THIS MATTER coming on to be heard and being heard by the Council of the
North Carolina State Bar pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code Chapter 1, Subchapter B, §
.0117(a) of the State Bar Discipline & Disability Rules upon an affidavit of tender of
surrender oflicense executed by Katherine Evans on Oct. 13, 1999 and filed in the offices
ofthe North Carolina State Bar on Oct. 13, 1999.
Based upon the affidavit, the COilllcil tlnds that Katherine Evans' conduct of
misappropriating client funds is grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. § 84­
28(b)(2) in that Katherine Evans violated Rules 8.4(b) and (c) ofthe Revised Rules of
Professional Conduct.
\VHEREFORE, upon Motion made and duly seconded, the Council enters the
following Order:
1. The tender of surrender of license of Katherine Evans is hereby accepted.
2. Katherine Evans is hereby is DISBARRED from the practice of law in North
Carolina.
3. Katherine Evans shall comply with the provisions of27 N.C.A.C. Chapter 1,
Subchapter B, § .OI24 of the State Bar Discipline & Disbarment Rules and shall
surrender her license and permanent membership card to the Secretary of the North
Carolina State Bar along with the affidavit required by § .0124 of the State Bar Discipline
& Disbarment Rules.
4. The costs of this action are taxed against Katherine Evans.
Done and ordered this 22nd day of October, 1999.

AFFIDAVIT OF SURRENDER

I, Katherine E. Evans, being duly sworn, depose and say as follows:


1.	 I hereby tender my license to practice law and desire to resign from the North Carolina State
Bar. This resignation is freely and voluntarily tendered, is not the result of coercion or duress
and I am fully aware of the implications of submitting this resignation.
2.	 I understand that State Bar Council will consider this affidavit of surrender at its meeting on
October 22, 1999 and I waive any further notice.
3.	 I am aware that there is pending an investigation into allegations that I misappropriated at
least $56,300 of client funds from a trust account that I maintained.
4.	 I acknowledge that the material facts upon which the grievance is predicated are true.
5.	 I am submitting this resignation because I know that if charges were predicated upon
misconduct under investigation, I could not successfully defend against them.
