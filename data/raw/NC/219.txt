CONSENT ORDER
OF
DISBARMENT

THIS MATTER came on to be heard and was heard by the undersigned Judge of the
Superior Court of Wake County. Petitioner, the North Carolina State Bar, was represented by
Katherine E. Jean. Respondent, Troy Anthony Smith, was represented by Christopher C. Fialko.
Based upon the Affidavit of Surrender of Law License submitted to the court by Troy
Anthony Smith, the court makes the following:

FINDINGS OF FACT
1. Troy Anthony Smith operated a law office in Waxhaw, North Carolina.
2. Smith has entered a plea of guilty in the United States District Court for the Western District
of North Carolina to the following criminal offenses: (a) engaging in a conspiracy with members
of Mortgage Fraud Cell No.1 to commit offenses against the United States, including making a
false statement in relation to a loan, engaging in mail fraud, engaging in wire fraud and engaging
in bank fraud, in violation of 18 U.S.C. 1014, 1341, 1343 and 1344; (b) engaging in a conspiracy
with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell No.2 to commit offenses against the United States,
including making a false statement in relation to a loan, engaging in mail fraud, engaging in wire
fraud and engaging in bank fraud, in violation of 18 U.S.C. 1014, 1341, 1343 and 1344; (c)
engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell No.1 to engage in monetary
transactions by through or to a fmancial institution, affecting interstate or foreign commerce, in
criminally derived property of a value greater than $10,000.00, such property having been
derived from a specified unlawful activity in violation of 18 U.S.C. 1957; and (d) engaging in a
conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell No.2 to engage in monetary transactions by
through or to a financial institution, affecting interstate or foreign commerce, in criminally
derived property of a value greater than $10,000.00, such property having been derived from a
specified unlawful activity in violation of 18 U.S.C. 1957.
3. Smith is in fact guilty of these charges.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the court makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
1. By (a) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud Cell No.1 to commit
offenses against the United States, including making a false statement in relation to a loan,
engaging in mail fraud, engaging in wire fraud and engaging in bank fraud, in violation of 18
U.S.C. 1014, 1341, 1343 and 1344; (b) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage
Fraud Cell No.2 to commit offenses against the United States, including making a false statement
in relation to a loan, engaging in mail fraud, engaging in wire fraud and engaging in bank fraud,
in violation of 18 U.S.C. 1014, 1341, 1343 and 1344; (c) engaging in a conspiracy with members
of Mortgage Fraud Cell No. 1 to engage in monetary transactions by through or to a financial
institution, affecting interstate or foreign commerce, in criminally derived property of a value
greater than $10,000.00, such property having been derived from a specified unlawful activity in
violation of 18 U.S.C. 1957; and (d) engaging in a conspiracy with members of Mortgage Fraud
Cell No.2 to engage in monetary transactions by through or to a financial institution, affecting
interstate or foreign commerce, in criminally derived property of a value greater than $10,000.00,
such property having been derived from a specified unlawful activity in violation of 18 U .S.C.
1957, Smith violated Rules 8.4(b) and (c) of the North Carolina Revised Rules of Professional
Conduct.

2. Smith's misconduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. Section 8428(b)(2).
IT IS NOW, THEREFORE, ORDERED:
1. Troy Anthony Smith is DISBARRED from the practice of law in North Carolina.
2. Troy Anthony Smith shall comply with the provisions of Subchapter B, Section .0124 of the
Rules and Regulations of the North Carolina State Bar and shall surrender his license and
permanent membership card to the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar along with the
affidavit required by Subchapter B, Section .0124.
3. The costs of this action are taxed against Troy Anthony Smith.
