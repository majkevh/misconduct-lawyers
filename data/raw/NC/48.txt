OF JAMES T. FERGUSON III

ORDER OF DISBARMENT

THIS MATTER coming on to be heard and being heard by the Council of
the North Carolina State Bar pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code Chapter 1,
Subchapter B, § .0117(a) of the State Bar Discipline & Disability Rules upon an
affidavit of tender of surrender of license executed by James T. Ferguson on
August 23, 2005 and filed in the offices of the North Carolina State Bar on
August 24, 2005.
Based upon the affidavit, the Council finds that the conduct of James T.
Ferguson III is grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. § 84-28(b)(2) in
that James T. Ferguson III engaged in serious criminal conduct in violation of
Rules 8.4(b) and (c) of the Revised Rules of Professional Conduct.
WHEREFORE, upon Motion made and duly seconded, the Council enters
the following Order:
I. The tender of surrender of license of James T. Ferguson III is hereby
accepted.
2. James T. Ferguson III is hereby is DISBARRED from the practice of
law in North Carolina.
3. James T. Ferguson III shall comply with the provisions 01'27 N.C.A.C.
Chapter 1, Subchapter B, § .0124 of the State Bar Discipline & Disbarment Rules
and shall surrender his license and permanent membership card to the Secretary of
the North Carolina State Bar along with the affidavit required by § .0124 of the
State Bar Discipline & Disbarment Rules.
4. The costs of this action are taxed against James T. Ferguson lII.
Done and ordered this 21st day of October

AFFIDAVIT OF SURRENDER

James E. Ferguson, III, being first duly sworn, deposes and says as follows:
I.
I desire to resign and hereby tender my license to practice law in North
Carolina pursuant to the provisions of 27 N.C. Admin. Code Chapter I, Subchapter B,
Section .0117 of the N.C. State Bar Discipline & Disability Rules.
2.
My resignation is freely and voluntarily tendered. It is not the result of

coercion or duress. I am fully aware of the implication of submitting my resignation.

3.
I am aware that there is pending an investigation arising out the following:
on July 28, 2005 I was convicted of one count of conspiracy to commit securities fraud,
mail fraud and wire fraud in violation of 15 U.S.c. Sec. 78(j)(b) upon a plea of guilty to
count I of an indictment in the United States District Court for the Western District of
North Carolina, file number 5:05-CR-207T.
4.
I acknowledge that certain material facts upon which my guilty plea was

based are true.

5.
I am submitting my resignation because I know that if disciplinary charges
were predicated upon my guilty plea, I could not successfully defend against them.
