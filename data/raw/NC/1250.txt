This matter is before the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar pursuant to a petition
for reinstatement filed on September 8, 1997 by David J. Turlington III.

It appears from a review of the order of discipline entered on January 29, 1997 that
petitioner was ordered suspended from the practice of law for 11 months, beginning as of
December 17, 1996.
It further appears from the verified petition that petitioner:
1. has complied with 27 NCAC 1B, § .0124 of the Discipline and Disability Rules of
the North Carolina State Bar;
2. has complied with all applicable orders of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission;
3. has not engaged in the unauthorized practice of law during the period of suspension of
his license; and
4. has not engaged in any conduct during the period of suspension constituting grounds
for discipline under NCGS § 84-28(b).
After conducting the necessary investigation regarding the compliance of the petitioner
with the requirements set forth in 27 NCAC lB, § .012S(b)(3), counsel for the North Carolina
State Bar has not filed any objection to the petition within the time required by 27 NCAC IB, §
.0 12S(b)(4).
THEREFORE, pursuant to 27 NCAC lB, § .Ol2S(b)(S) of the Discipline and Disability
Rules of the North Carolina State Bar, it is hereby ORDERED:
David J. Turlington III is reinstated to the active practice of law in North Carolina as of
the dale of this order.
