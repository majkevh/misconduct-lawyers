THIS MATTER came before the Chair of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission
pursuant to 27 N.C.A.C. IB § .0117(d) upon an Affidavit of Surrender of Law License
executed by Stephen R. Melvin dated 6 June 2013 and filed with the Clerk of the
Disciplinary Hearing Commission on 10 June 2013.
Based upon the pleadings and the record, including the Affidavit of Surrender of
Law License, the undersigned makes the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1.
Defendant, Stephen R. Melvin, was licensed to practice law in North
Carolina in August 1981.
2.
During all periods relevant hereto, Defendant was engaged in the practice
oflaw in Fayetteville, Cumberland County, North Carolina.
3.
In this disciplinary action the State Bar has alleged that Melvin used
entrusted funds for the benefit of himself and/or third parties without authorization from
the beneficial owners of the funds.
4.
In his Affidavit of Surrender, Melvin admits the truth of the allegations
against him and acknowledges that he cannot successfully defend against those
allegations.
5.
Defendant has indicated his wish to consent to disbrument by filing an
Affidavit of Surrender with the Disciplinary Hearing Commission. The affidavit meets
the requirements set f011h in N.C.A.C. IB § .0117(a)(1), (2), (3), & (4) and § .0117(d).
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the undersigned makes the following:

. 1.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

l.
27 N .C.A.C. IB § .0108 provides that the Chair of the Disciplinary
Hearing Commission has the power to enter orders disbarring members by consent.
2.
Defendant's affidavit meets the requirements set forth in N.C.A.C. IB §
.0117(a)(1), (2), (3), & (4) and § .0117(d), and the facts upon which the affidavit is
predicated warrant Defendant's disbarment.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law, the
undersigned Chair of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission enters the following
ORDER
I.

The surrender of the law license of Stephen R. Melvin is hereby accepted.

2.
Stephen R. Melvin is DISBARRED from the practice of law in North
Carolina effective upon the entry of this order with the Secretary of the State Bar.
3.
Defendant shall comply with Rule .0124 of the State Bar Discipline and
Disability Rules, 27 N.C.A.C. IB § .0124.
4.

The costs of this action are taxed against Defendant.

This the ~ day of June, 2013.
