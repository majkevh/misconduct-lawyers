THIS MATTER came before the undersigned Judge of the Superior Court of
Wake County upon the tender of surrender of his law license by Sean Nelson Rogers
Wells.
Based upon the Affidavit of Surrender of Law License submitted to the Court by
Respondent, Sean Nelson Rogers Wells (hereinafter "Wells"), the Court makes the
following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
1.
Wells was admitted to the North Carolina State Bar in 2006 and is, and
was at all times referred to herein, an attorney at law licensed to practice in North
Carolina, subject to the rules, regulations, and Rules of Professional Conduct of the North
Carolina State Bar and the laws of the State of North Carolina.
2.
Wells is aware that there is presently pending an investigation regarding
allegations that he is guilty of misappropriation of client funds.
3.
Wells desires to resign his license to practice law and has tendered his
license to practice law in North Carolina.
4.
Wells' resignation is freely and voluntarily rendered. It is not the result of
coercion or duress. Wells is fully aware of the implication of submitting his resignation.
5.
Wells aclmowledges that the material facts upon which the investigation is
predicated are true.
6.
Wells has submitted his resignation because he knows that if disciplinary
charges were predicated upon the misconduct under investigation, he could not
successfully defend against them.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the court makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
I.
By misappropriating client funds, Wells engaged in criminal acts that
reflect adversely on his honesty, trustworthiness or fitness as a lawyer in other respects in
violation of Rule 8.4(b) of the Rules of Professional Conduct and engaged in conduct
involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation in violation of Rule 8.4(c) of the
Rules of Professional Conduct.
2.
Wells' misconduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C.
Gen. Stat. ยง 84-28(b)(2).
3.
The courts of this State have the inherent authority to talee disciplinary
action against attorneys licensed to practice law in North Carolina.
4.
This Court has jurisdiction over the person of Sean Nelson Rogers Wells
and of the subject matter of this proceeding.
5.

Wells has engaged in professional misconduct warranting disbarment.

IT IS NOW, THEREFORE, ORDERED:
Sean Nelson Rogers Wells is DISBARRED from the practice oflaw in
North Carolina. This disbarment is effective upon the entry of this order by the court.
1.

2.
Wells shall comply with the provisions of27 N.C. Admin. Code, Chapter

Bar and shall surrender his license and permanent membership card to the Secretary of
the North Carolina State Bar along with the affidavit required by ยง .0124. Wells has 30
days from the entry of this order to complete the requirements of ยง .0124.
3.
Wells shall not petition for reinstatement of his law license until the
expiration of at least five (5) years from the date of entry ofthis order.
4.
Wells' law license shall not be reinstated l1.I1til he has demonstrated to the
