THIS MATTER came before the undersigned Judge of the Superior Court of
Wake County. Petitioner, the North Carolina State Bar, was represented by Carmen K.
Hoyme. Respondent, Michael A. Williams, was represented by Steven B. DeCillis.
Based upon the Mfidavit of Surrender of Law License submitted to the Court by
Respondent, Michael A. Williams (hereinafter "Williams"), the Court makes the
following:
FINDINGS OF FACT

Williams was admitted to the North Carolina State Bar in 2001 and is, and
was at all times referred to herein, an attorney at law licensed to practice in North
Carolina, subject to the rules, regulations, and Rules of Professional Conduct of the North
Carolina State Bar and the laws of the State of North Carolina.
1.

?
During the periods relevant hereto, Williams was engaged in the practice
of law in Warrenton, Warren County, North Carolina.

3.
Williams misappropriated entrusted funds in the amount of at least
$16,350.00 without authorization from the beneficial owners of the funds.
4.
A review of trust account and bank records reveals that Williams
deposited the full $16,350.00 back into trust and voluntarily notified the North Carolina
State Bar of his misconduct, prior to the filing of this action.

Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the Court makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

1.
By misappropriating client funds from his trust account, Williams used
entrusted funds for his personal benefit in violation of Rule 1.15-20) of the Rules of
Professional Conduct and engaged in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or
misrepresentation in violation of Rule 8.4(c) of the Rules of Professional Conduct.
?
Williams' misconduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C.
Gen. Stat. ยง 84-28(b)(2).

3.
The courts of this State have the inherent authority to take disciplinary
action against attorneys licensed to practice law in North Carolina.
4.
This Court has jurisdiction over the person of Michael A. Williams and of
the subject matter of this proceeding.
5.

Williams has engaged in professional misconduct warranting disbarment.

AFFIDAVIT OF SURRENDER

MICHAEL A. WILLIAMS, being first duly sworn, deposes and says as follows:
1. I desire to resign and hereby tender my license to practice law in North Carolina.

') My resignation is freely and voluntarily rendered, and is not the result of coercion or duress.
I am fully aware of the implications of submitting my resignation.
3. I am aware that there is a pending investigation by the State Bar regarding allegations that I
misappropriated approximately $16,350.00 from my attorney trust account that had been entrusted to
me in a fiduciary capacity and utilized those funds for my own benefit without authorization from the
beneficial owners of the funds.
4. I returned the full $16,350.00 back into my trust account and voluntarily notified the North
Carolina State Bar of my misconduct, prior to the filing of this action.
5. I acknowledge that the material facts upon which the investigation is predicated are true.
6. I am submitting my resignation because I know that if disciplinary charges were predicated
upon the misconduct under investigation, I could not successfully defend against them.
