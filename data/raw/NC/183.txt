THIS MATTER came on to be heard and was heard by the undersigned Judge of the
Superior Court of Wake County. Petitioner, the North Carolina State Bar was represented by
Katherine E. Jean. Respondent, Thomas D. Brown, was represented by Prosser D. Carnegie.
Based upon the Affidavit of Surrender of Law License submitted to the court by Brown,
the court makes the following:

FINDINGS OF FACT
I. Thomas D. Brown utilized at least $970,000.00 held by him in trust, for his own benefit or for
the benefit of third parties without authorization to do so from the beneficial owners of the funds.
2. Thomas D. Brown misappropriated entrusted funds in the amount of at least $970,000.00.
Based upon the foregoing Findings of Fact, the court makes the following:

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
I. By misappropriating entrusted funds for his own personal benefit or for the benefit of third
parties without authorization to do so from the beneficial owners of the funds, Thomas D. Brown
violated Rules I.IS-2(j) and 8.4(c) of the Revised Rules of Professional Conduct.
2. Thomas D. Brown's misconduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuan1 to N.C. Gen. Stat.
Section 84-28(b)(2).
IT IS NOW, THEREFORE, ORDERED:
1. Thomas D. Brown is DISBARRED from the practice oflaw in North Carolina.

2. Thomas D. Brown shall comply with the provisions of Subchapter B, Section .0024 of the
Rules and Regulations of the North Carolina State Bar and shall surrender his license and

permanent membership card to the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar along with the
affidavit required by Subchapter B, Section .0024.
3. The costs of this action are taxed against Thoma D. Brown.
