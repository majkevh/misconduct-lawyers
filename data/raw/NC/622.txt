RONNA DAWN GIBBS, Attorney

THIS MATTER was presented to a hearing panel of the Disciplinary Hearing
Commission composed of Sharon B. Alexander, Chair, Renny W. Deese, and Randy A. Moreau
upon the consent of the parties. Margaret Cloutier represented the North Carolina State Bar and
Dudley A. Witt represented Defendant Ronna Dawn Gibbs. Based upon the parties' consent, the
hearing panel makes the following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
I. By order entered December 1,2005, the Chair of the Grievance Committee transferred
Ronna Dawn Gibbs to disability inactive status.
2. On December 5, 2013, Philip H. Lavine, M. D. stated his opinion that Gibbs is in
remission from major depression and does not currently suffer from post-traumatic stress
symptoms. Dr. Lavine opined that Gibbs is fit to resume the practice of law.
3. On March 13, 2014, Gibbs filed a Petition for Reinstatement to Active Status.
4. The North Carolina State Bar does not have evidence contrary to the opinion of Dr.
Levine.
BASED UPON the foregoing Findings of Fact, the hearing panel enters the following:
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW
I. This hearing panel has jurisdiction over the parties and the subject matter.
2. Gibbs has satisfied all of the conditions of27 N.C.A.C. IB ยง.0125(c)(3) to be eligible
to return to active status.

THEREFORE, the hearing panel herewith GRANTS Gibbs' Petition for Reinstatement to
Active Status.
