THIS MATTER was presented to a panel of the Disciplinary Hearing Commission
composed of Theodore C. Edwards, II, Chair, Ronald R. Davis, and Karen B. Ray upon the
consent ofthe parties. A. Root Edmonson represents the North Carolina State Bar and
Jacqueline C. Morris-Goodson is pro se. Based upon the parties consent, the panel makes the
following:
FINDINGS OF FACT
I. By consent order entered March 17, 2003, the Chair of the Grievance Committee of
the North Carolina State Bar in 02G0742 transferred Jacqueline C. Morris-Goodson ("MorrisGoodson") to disability inactive status due to major depressive disorder, recurrent anxiety and
chronic pain syndrome that significantly impaired Morris-Goodson's professional judgment,
perfornlance and competence as an attorney.
2. On June 28, 20 II, Morris-Goodson filed a Petition for Reinstatement to Active Status
fi'om Disability Inactive Status.
3. On February 18, 2003, Dr. Allan Maltbie, a psychiatrist licensed in NC, provided the
professional opinion that formed the basis for Morris-Goodson's transfer to disability inactive
statliS. Dr. Maltbie treated Morris-Goodson from 2003 until he retired in 2007. Dr. Maltbie did
follow-up assessments to reevaluate Morris-Goodson's disability status on May 25,2010 and
March 15,2011. Based upon these two assessments, Dr. Maltbie is ofthe opinion that MonisGoodson is no longer symptomatically depressed and no longer suffers a disabling condition. Dr.
Tiona Praylow has provided Morris-Goodson with psychotherapy since April, 20 I O. Dr. Ellliua

would significantly impair her return to the profession.

Maltbie, Praylow and Williams.
