) ORDER OF DISBARMENT

THIS MATTER coming on to be heard and being heard by the Council of the
North Carolina State Bar pursuant to 27 N.C. Admin. Code Chapter I, Subchapter B,
Rule. 0 I 17 of the Discipline and Disability Rules of the North Carolina State Bar, upon
an affidavit of tender oflicense pursuant to Rule .0117(a) executed by David F. Tamer
and filed in the offices of the North Carolina State Bar on March 8, 2000.
Based upon the affidavit, the Council finds that the conduct of David F. Tamer
is grounds for discipline pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. Section 84-28(b)(2) in that David F.
Tamer misappropriated fiduciary funds in violation of Rule US-lea), Rule 8A(b) and
Rule 8.4(c) of the North Carolina State Bar Revised Rules of Professional Conduct.
WHEREFORE, upon motion duly made and seconded, the Council enters the
following order:
1. The tender of surrender of license of David F. Tamer is accepted.
2. David F. Tamer is DISBARRED from the practice of law in North Carolina.
3. David F. Tamer shall forthwith surrender his license and membership card to
the Secretary. He shall comply with all provisions of27 N.C. Admin. Code, Chapter 1,
Rule .0124 ofthe Discipline and Disability Rules of the North Carolina State Bar.
4. The costs of this action are hereby taxed against David F. Tamer.
Done and ordered this the 14th day of April, 2000.

AFFIDAVIT

To: The Council of the North Carolina State Bar
1. I, David F. Tamer, hereby tender my law license and desire to resign from the
North Carolina State Bar. My resignation is freely and voluntarily rendered. It is not the
result of coercion or duress. I am fully aware of the implications of submitting my
resignation.
2. I am aware that there is pending an investigation or other proceeding regarding
allegations that I misappropriated funds from three estates wherein I served as the
administrator.
3. I acknowledge that the facts forming the basis of the investigation or other
proceeding and referenced herein are true.
4. I submit my resignation because I know if charges were predicated upon the
misconduct under investigation, I could not successfully defend against them.
