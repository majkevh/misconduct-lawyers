ORDER OF DISBARMENT

THIS MATTER was heard by the Council of the North Carolina State Bar pursuant to 27
N.C.A.C. 1B §.0117 (a) of the State Bar Discipline and Disability Rules upon the affidavit of
sUlTender of license executed by Jonathan A. McCollum and filed in the offices of the State Bar
on 22 October 2010.
Based on the affidavit, the Council finds that Jonathan A. McCollum forged two
documents purporting to be judicial orders, made misrepresentations to his clients about those
documents, and initially made false statements in response to the State Bar's inquiry about those
documents. In connection with this conduct, McCollum pled guilty to misdemeanor forgery in
Wake County file no. IOCR 208059. This conduct constitutes grounds for discipline pursuant to
N.C. Gen. Stat. § 84-28 (b)(1) and (b)(2), in that McCollum was convicted of a criminal offense
showing professional unfitness and violated Rule 8.4 of the Rules of Professional Conduct.
WHEREFORE, upon motion made and duly seconded, the Council enters the following
Order:
I. The tender of surrender of the license of Jonathan A. McCollum is hereby accepted.
2. Jonathan A. McCollum is hereby DISBARRED from the practice oflaw in North
Carolina.
3. Jonathan A. McCollum shall comply with the provisions of27 NCAC 1B § .0124 of
the State Bar Discipline and Disability Rules and shall surrender his license and
permanent membership card to the Secretary of the North Carolina State Bar along
with the affidavit required by 27 NCAC IB § .0124 of the State Bar Discipline and
Disability Rules.
4. The costs of this action are taxed against Jonathan A. McCollum.
Done and ordered this the 29th day of October,
