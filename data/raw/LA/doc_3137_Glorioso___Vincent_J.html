NUMBER:  04-DB-059

RECOMMENDATION TO THE LOUISIANA SUPREME COURT

INTRODUCTION AND PROCEDURAL HISTORY

  This petition for reinstatement was filed by Vincent J. Glorioso, Jr. ("petitioner" or "Mr. Glorioso") on May 3, 2004.  The petitioner was suspended from the practice of law by the Louisiana Supreme Court on June 6, 2002 based upon his misconduct involving the conversion of third party funds, conflicts of interest, failure to provide full disclosure in a business transaction with a client, and the commingling of personal and client funds in his trust account to avoid seizure of his personal funds by the IRS.  See In re Glorioso, 2002-1074 (La. 6/6/02); 818 So.2d 320.

  The petition for reinstatement was forwarded to the Office of Disciplinary Counsel ("ODC") by the Board Administrator's Office on May 3, 2004.  ODC filed an answer to the petition for reinstatement on July 14, 2004 in which it took no position with respect to the petition and requested that the matter be fixed for hearing.  The hearing in this matter was held on August 17, 2004.  The hearing committee issued its report on September 8, 2004, recommending that the petition for reinstatement be granted. 

  Neither party filed a response to the hearing committee's report. Oral argument before Panel "C" of the Disciplinary Board was held on October 28, 2004.  Deputy Disciplinary Counsel Robert S. Kennedy, Jr. appeared on behalf of ODC.  The petitioner and his counsel, John R. Martzell, also appeared.

DISCUSSION

I.  The Reinstatement Hearing

Stipulations entered into by the parties at the hearing establish that the petitioner has satisfied all the criteria of Rule XIX, Section 24(E).  These criteria are as follows:

(1)  The petitioner has fully complied with the terms and conditions of all prior disciplinary orders;

(2)  The petitioner has not engaged or attempted to engage in the unauthorized practice of law during his period of suspension;

(3)  The petitioner was not suffering under a physical or mental disability or infirmity at the time of the suspension;

(4)  The petitioner recognizes the wrongfulness and seriousness of the misconduct for which he was suspended;

(5)  The petitioner has not engaged in any other professional misconduct since his suspension;

(6) Notwithstanding the conduct for which the petitioner was disciplined, the petitioner has the requisite honesty and integrity to practice law;

(7) The petitioner has kept informed about recent developments in the law and is competent to practice and has satisfied the MCLE requirements for the year of reinstatement;

(8)  The petitioner has paid to the Louisiana State Bar Association currently owed bar dues;

(9)  The petitioner has paid all filing fees owed to the Clerk of Court and all disciplinary costs to the Disciplinary Board;

(10)  The petitioner has paid to the Disciplinary Board currently owed disciplinary administration and enforcement fees required under Section 8(A) of this rule and has filed the registration statement under Section 8(C) of this rule.

See Tr., pp. 14-15;

ODC's proposed stipulations contained in its pre-hearing memorandum filed on August 2, 2004[1]; and petitioner's proposed stipulations (numbers 4-7) contained in his pre-hearing memorandum filed on August 6, 2004[2].

In further support of his petition, Mr. Glorioso offered testimony at the hearing from:  Russ M. Herman, a New Orleans attorney and professional and social acquaintance of the petitioner; Maria Glorioso, petitioner's daughter, a New Orleans attorney, and his employer; and Neal K. Adler, petitioner's CPA.  Petitioner also testified in support of his petition.

Mr. Herman testified as to the petitioner's good character and reputation and petitioner's willingness to accept responsibility for his misconduct.  Tr. pp. 8-10. Ms. Glorioso testified that petitioner currently works as a paralegal in her office.  Tr. p. 20.  She further testified that, assuming petitioner was reinstated to the practice of law, he would not have access to clients' funds. Tr. pp. 20-23.  Petitioner would be an employee of the firm, and all cases would belong to the firm.  Tr. pp. 23, 25. 

Mr. Adler testified that if the clients' funds were placed in the Glorioso Law Firm trust account, the funds would not be subject to a lien for any of the petitioner's tax liability.  Tr. pp. 29, 31.  He further testified that the petitioner was current with all his federal income tax filings and that he had received a valid extension to file his 2003 return until October 15, 2004.[3] While petitioner has not paid all taxes due under these filings, the IRS has not issued any notices of deficiency or lien of enforcement against the petitioner.  Tr. pp. 29-30.  Only his personal earnings found in his personal bank accounts would be subject to seizure by the IRS.  Tr. pp. 31-32.  

The petitioner testified that he has made restitution in all of the underlying disciplinary matters, that he only has a personal checking account, and that, if reinstated, he will not have any professional accounts.  All clients' funds will go into the Glorioso Law Firm trust account. Tr. p. 38-39.  He also testified that he anticipates his tax problems with the IRS to be over within a year.  Tr. p. 49.[4]  He stated that he will remain an employee of the Glorioso Law Firm and that any cases he works on will be property of the firm.  Tr. p. 44.  Petitioner also stated that he will never get into a "business arrangement" with a client again.  Tr. p. 46-47.  Moreover, the petitioner submitted an affidavit and numerous letters from lawyers who wrote to ODC attesting to his good character and reputation.

  Based on the above, the Board finds that the hearing committee correctly concluded that the petitioner met the criteria of Section 24(E).  Petitioner also established that his current tax liability is not a threat to any funds belonging to the clients or prospective clients of the Glorioso Law Firm and that he will not engage in improper "business arrangements" with clients in the future.  The Board therefore recommends that his petition for reinstatement be granted.

RECOMMENDATION

  The Disciplinary Board adopts the hearing committee's recommendation and recommends that Mr. Glorioso's petition for reinstatement be granted.  The petitioner has met the criteria under Rule XIX, Section 24(E) for reinstatement.

CONCURRING OPINION

  I reluctantly concur with the panel's recommendation in this matter.  I have serious concerns regarding whether the petitioner should be readmitted to practice while he has an obligation to the IRS for $120,000.00 in income taxes, plus penalties and interest.  Not having the benefit of the transcript of his testimony, I understand from footnote 1 of the panel's recommendation that the petitioner thinks that the bulk of his IRS problems have been discharged in bankruptcy, although no one can give him an "absolute fair answer."  It is difficult to have an IRS income tax lien discharged in bankruptcy, although it can be done.  I would feel much more comfortable if the panel recommended that the Supreme Court defer acting on Mr. Glorioso's Petition for Reinstatement until satisfactory evidence is furnished that he has, indeed fully resolved his problems with the IRS.  I am not, however, prepared to dissent if the majority of the board feels otherwise.

  Respectfully submitted,

  RICHARD E. GERARD, JR.
