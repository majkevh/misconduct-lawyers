PASTOR, ROBERT J.
362 So.2d 486 (La. 1978)

   

SUPREME COURT OF LOUISIANA
No. 60161
IN RE: Robert J. PASTOR

PETITION FOR LEAVE TO SURRENDER LICENSE TO PRACTICE LAW AND

TO HAVE STRICKEN HIS NAME FROM THE ROLLS OF

ATTORNEYS FOR THE PERIOD OF TWO (2) YEARS

     **SUSPENSION ON CONSENT**

June 9, 1978

TO THE HONORABLE SUPREME COURT OF THE STATE OF LOUISIANA

THROUGH THE COMMITTEE ON PROFESSIONAL

RESPONSIBILITY OF THE LOUISIANA STATE

BAR ASSOCIATION

      The Petition of Robert J. Pastor, Respondent herein, appearing herein personally and through Ron S. Macaluso, his attorney, with respect represents.

That Robert J. Pastor was admitted to practice law in the State of Louisiana October 5, 1973.

That Robert J. Pastor, a member of the Louisiana State Bar Association, was on December 7, 1976, found guilty of the offense of accessory after the fact of attempted possession of cocaine in violation of R.S. 14:25 (Accessory After the Fact) and R.S. 40:967 (Possession) in the Fifteenth Judicial District Court for the Parish of Lafayette, Louisiana, under Docket No. 41-525, all as set out in the true copy of Judgment and Minute Entry attached to "Petition for Disciplinary Action" filed against Respondent in this Court on October 26, 1977, under the above Docket No. 60,161, which is made part hereof by reference.

That the crime of which Respondent stands convicted is a felony and a serious crime and that said conviction is final due to Respondent's plea of guilty.

That a "Petition for Disciplinary Action" was filed against Respondent on October 26, 1977, under Docket Number 60,161 of the Supreme Court.

That Robert J. Pastor acknowledges that his conviction was of a serious offense, a felony;  and that his conviction is final.

That Respondent is presently undergoing professional medical and psychiatric treatment in an attempt to regain his capacity and acceptability to practice law.

That Robert J. Pastor herein petitions this Court for leave to surrender his license to practice law for a period of two (2) years from date of this Court's Order, thus resulting in his suspension for that period by his own consent.

That further, Respondent requests that reinstatement not be automatic but will provide that Respondent may apply for reinstatement after the lapse of said two (2) year period;  and that such application shall be granted by this Court, after due proceedings, upon a clear showing by respondent that he is emotionally and morally able to resume the practice of law.  Absent reinstatement by this Court, Respondent understands his suspension to practice law shall continue in effect.

WHEREFORE, Robert J. Pastor prays that he be allowed to surrender his license to practice law and that same be revoked and cancelled, further, that his name be stricken from the rolls of attorneys authorized to practice law in the State of Louisiana, all for a period of two (2) years, provided further that his reinstatement will not be automatic but will provide that Respondent may apply for reinstatement after the lapse of said two (2) year period;  and that such application shall be granted by this Court, after due proceedings, upon a clear showing by respondent that he is emotionally and morally able to resume the practice of law.  Absent reinstatement by this Court, Respondent understands his suspension to practice law shall continue in effect.

[S] Robert J. Pastor

[S] Ron S. Macaluso, Counsel

STATE OF LOUISIANA

PARISH OF  TANGIPAHOA

BEFORE ME, the undersigned authority, a Notary Public, duly commissioned and qualified, personally came and appeared ROBERT J. PASTOR who after being by me duly sworn did depose and say:

(1) That his resignation and surrender of his license to practice law in the State of Louisiana for the period of two (2) years under the conditions set forth in the above and attached "Petition" to do so is freely and voluntarily made;  he is not being subjected to coercion or duress;  and he is fully aware of the implications of submitting to such suspension;  and

(2) That he acknowledges and affirms under oath all statements contained in his Petition for suspension on consent;

(3) That he acknowledges and understands that this suspension is solely in connection with his convictions of the offense in violation of R.S. 14:25 (Accessory After the Fact) and R.S. 40:967 (Possession) growing out of Case No. 41-525 of the Fifteenth Judicial District Court for the Parish of Lafayette, Louisiana;  and that the "RECOMMENDED APPROVAL" by the Louisiana State Bar Association's Committee on Professional Responsibility does not foreclose or prevent action by the Louisiana State Bar Association's Committee on Professional Responsibility on any separate and additional misconduct and/or convictions of himself that comes to the attention of the Louisiana State Bar Association's Committee on Professional Responsibility;

(4) That he submits his suspension on consent because he knows that were proceedings pursued to completion, he could not successfully defend against them.

[S] ROBERT J. PASTOR

SWORN TO AND SUBSCRIBED

BEFORE ME, THIS  8 DAY OF  June , 1978.

[S] Richard C. Macaluso

  NOTARY PUBLIC

ORDER

The foregoing Petition, the premises, and the law considered:

IT IS ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED

That ROBERT J. PASTOR is by his consent suspended from the practice of law, and his name stricken from the Roll of Attorneys authorized to practice law for the period of two (2) years.

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, ADJUDGED, AND DECREED

That reinstatement of Respondent will not be automatic but Respondent may apply for reinstatement after the lapse of said two (2) year period;  and that such application shall be granted by this Court, after due proceedings, upon a clear showing by Respondent that he is emotionally and morally able to resume the practice of law.  Absent reinstatement by this Court, Respondent understands his suspension to practice law shall continue in effect.

New Orleans, Louisiana, this 29th day of June, 1978.

/s/ Joe W. Sanders     CHIEF JUSTICE
