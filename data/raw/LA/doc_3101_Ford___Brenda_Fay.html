FORD-JONES, BRENDA F.
99-DB-002 (4/21/1999) Failure To Pay Third-Party Providers  Reprimand 

LOUISIANA ATTORNEY DISCIPLINARY BOARD

NUMBER 99-DB-002

IN RE: BRENDA F. FORD-JONES

RULING OF THE DISCIPLINARY BOARD

   This is a disciplinary proceeding based on the filing of a petition for consent discipline by Brenda F. Ford-Jones prior to the filing of formal charges.

PROCEDURAL HISTORY

   On January 22, 1999, Respondent and the Office of Disciplinary Counsel filed a joint petition for consent discipline, prior to the filing of formal charges.  The petition states that Respondent failed to notify and deliver funds claimed by a medical provider, notwithstanding a letter of guarantee.  It is agreed that Respondent's behavior violates Rule 1.15(b).  The Office of Disciplinary Counsel also filed a concurrence on January 22, 1999.

   A panel of the Disciplinary Board reviewed this matter on February 25, 1999.  Respondent did not appear and the Office of Disciplinary Counsel was represented by Deputy Counsel Julie B. White

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

   Respondent failed to deliver all the funds claimed by a medical provider, despite executing a letter of guarantee in favor of the provider.  The doctor's bill totaled $6,023.75 and Respondent paid only $1000; the remainder of the settlement funds was disbursed to the client.  This complaint involves a violation of the Rules of Professional Conduct 1.15.

APPLICATION OF FACTORS TO BE CONSIDERED IN IMPOSING SANCTIONS

   Louisiana Supreme Court Rule XIX '10(C) states that in imposing a sanction after a finding of lawyer misconduct, the court or board shall consider the following factors:

   (1)   whether the lawyer has violated a duty owed to a client, to the public, to the legal system, or to  the profession;

   (2)   whether the lawyer acted intentionally, knowingly, or negligently;

   (3)   the amount of actual or potential injury caused by the lawyer's misconduct; and

   (4)   the existence of any aggravating or mitigating factors.

   The  Board finds that Respondent is guilty of the misconduct set forth in petition for consent discipline by clear and convincing evidence.  The Board finds that Respondent has violated duties owed to her client, and has engaged in negligent misconduct. 

   The Board finds the following A.B.A. Standards '9.22 aggravating circumstance to be present: substantial experience in the practice of law.  The A.B.A. Standards '9.32 mitigating factors are a cooperative attitude toward these proceedings; an absence of dishonest and selfish motive; good faith efforts at restitution and no prior discipline. 

   In consent disciplines, the Board may reject proposed discipline as too lenient, but when Respondent and disciplinary counsel have agreed that a violation has occurred, it may not reject consent discipline claiming that there was no violation.  See In re: Lieberman, 95-2628 (La. 6/7/96); 675 So.2d 272.  The Disciplinary Board therefore orders that Respondent, Brenda F. Ford-Jones, be issued a public reprimand and further orders that she be assessed with all costs and expenses of these proceedings, with legal interest to commence running thirty days from the date of finality of this Ruling until paid.
