ALDIGE, HUGH D.
00-DB-118 (06/29/2001) Reprimand Conflict of Interest

LOUISIANA ATTORNEY DISCIPLINARY BOARD

IN RE: HUGH D. ALDIGE

NUMBER 00-DB-118

RULING OF THE DISCIPLINARY BOARD

This is a disciplinary proceeding based on the filing of a Petition for Consent Discipline (herein "Petition") by the respondent, Hugh D. Aldige (herein "Respondent"), of Metairie, Louisiana, prior to the filing of formal charges. The Board accepts the Petition and orders a public reprimand be issued against Respondent.

PROCEDURAL HISTORY

As a result of two complaints received from clients of Respondent, the Office of Disciplinary Counsel (herein "ODC") initiated investigative matters numbers 0008292 and 0007740. In response to these investigations, prior to the institution of formal charges, Respondent filed a Petition for Consent Discipline and a Stipulation of Facts on September 20, 2000. On the same day, ODC filed a concurrence with the Petition. On September 28, 2000, a panel of the Disciplinary Board reviewed the documents filed in this matter.

STIPULATED FACTS

Helmker Complaint

According to the stipulated facts filed by Respondent, Mr. Helmker retained Respondent to assist him in a criminal matter. The division of the fee and all attorneys who would be assisting in the representation were not clearly delineated to the client in writing. In connection with this matter, Respondent admits to violating Rules 1.5[i] and 1.7[ii] of the Rules of Professional Conduct, by not properly having a division of fees between lawyers who were not in the same firm.

Cefalu Complaint

In September 1998, ODC received a complaint on behalf of a Mr. Cefalu. In 1993, Mr. Cefalu retained Respondent to defend him from a suit by American Medical Supply, Inc. for a disputed debt.

In January 1996, Respondent took action to judicially reinscribe a judgment on a note in an unrelated matter. At the time, the current owner of the note was D'Aquin Property, solely owned by Respondent's wife. The reinscription was done before Respondent withdrew from the earlier representation. Respondent admits no discussion was made to either client regarding possible conflicts in the matter in violation of Rule 1.7 of the Rules of Professional Conduct.[iii]

PETITION FOR CONSENT DISCIPLINE

Respondent tenders a conditional admission to the misconduct in exchange for a stated form of discipline, to wit, a public reprimand, with the following special conditions:

a. Respondent shall enroll in and attend one full day of Ethics School administered by the LSBA's Practice Assistance and Improvement Committee;

b.  Respondent shall cooperate completely with any and all investigations by the Office of Disciplinary Counsel and promptly respond to any and all complaints; and

c. Respondent shall refrain from engaging in any violation of the Rules of Professional Conduct.

APPLICATION OF FACTORS CONSIDERED IN IMPOSING SANCTIONS

Louisiana Supreme Court Rule XIX, Section 10(C) states that in imposing a sanction after a finding of lawyer misconduct, the Court or Board shall consider the following factors:

(1) whether the lawyer has violated a duty owed to a client, to the public, to the legal system, or  to the profession;

(2) whether the lawyer acted intentionally, knowingly, or negligently;

(3) the amount of actual or potential injury caused by the lawyer's misconduct; and

(4) the existence of any aggravating or mitigating factors.

The Board finds that Respondent's actions violated duties owed to his clients. Respondent asserts that his actions in this matter were unintentional. While the documents filed in this matter do not specify what harm, if any, was suffered by Respondent's clients, Respondent further asserts that he has taken steps to "rectify any harm he may have caused his prior clients."

Aggravating factors were not identified to the Board. Respondent notes that "in his thirty-two (32) years of practicing law, [Respondent] has never been sanctioned or disciplined." The Board finds the mitigating factor of absence of a prior disciplinary record [Standard 9.32(a)] and the aggravating factor of substantial experience in the practice of law [Standard 9.22(i)] are present in this matter. The mitigating factor of timely good faith effort to make restitution or to rectify consequences of misconduct is present [Standard 9.32(d)].

ANALYSIS

I. ABA Standards

The Louisiana Supreme Court relies on the ABA's Standards for Imposing Lawyer Sanction (herein "Standard") to determine the baseline sanction by "the type of duty violated, the lawyer's mental state and the extent of the injury caused; and then adjust the sanction in accordance with the aggravating and mitigating factors present." In re Quaid, 94-1316 (La. 11/30/94); 646 So. 2d 343, 350.

Standard 4.63 provides for reprimand when "a lawyer negligently fails to provide a client with accurate or complete information, and causes injury or potential injury to the client." In the Helmker matter, Respondent admits to failing to properly have a division of fees between lawyers who were not in the same firm, and failing to clearly delineate this information to the client in writing. As noted, the nature of the injury to Mr. Helmker is not outlined in the documents submitted by Respondent. However, the potential for injury exists when an attorney neglects his duty to provide a client with complete information.

Standard 4.33 provides for reprimand when "a lawyer is negligent in determining whether the representation of a client may be materially affected by the lawyer's own interests, or whether the representation will adversely affect another client, and causes injury or potential injury to a client." In the Cefalu matter, Respondent admits that he failed to discuss with Mr. Cefalu the existence of possible conflicts between his representation of Mr. Cefalu and his representation of the owner of the note. While the documents submitted in this matter fail to elaborate upon any injury sustained by these clients, the potential for injury to either or both clients existed.

Based upon the ABA guidelines, reprimand is an appropriate baseline sanction for Respondent's violation of Rules 1.5, and 1.7 of the Rules of Professional Conduct.

II.  Relevant Case Law

The Board also looks to relevant case law for assistance in determining an appropriate sanction for violations of the Rules of Professional Conduct. However, as noted by ODC, "finding jurisprudence that will mirror a disciplinary case is difficult, if not impossible . . . ." Research into cases of attorney misconduct involving violations of Rules 1.5 and 1.7 has failed to disclose any cases that are factually similar to the instant matter. The cases identified by this research seem to be limited to situations involving conduct on the part of the attorney more egregious than is present in this matter.

III.   Conclusion

The commentary to Standard 2.5 states that, "A reprimand is appropriate in cases where the lawyer's conduct, although violating ethical standards, is not serious enough to warrant suspension or disbarment." The ABA guidelines applying this principle suggest that the proposed sanction is appropriate for Respondent's violation of Rules 1.5 and 1.7 of the Rules of Professional Conduct. Furthermore, the jurisprudence of the Court suggests that the nature of Respondent's misconduct in this matter does not warrant the imposition of a harsher sanction.

RULING

Based on the foregoing, it is ordered that the petition for consent discipline be accepted, and that a public reprimand be issued against the respondent, Hugh D. Aldige, with the following special conditions:

a. Respondent shall enroll in and attend one full day of Ethics School administered by the LSBA's Practice Assistance and Improvement Committee;

b.  Respondent shall cooperate completely with any and all investigations by the Office of Disciplinary Counsel and promptly respond to any and all complaints; and

c. Respondent shall refrain from engaging in any violation of the Rules of Professional Conduct.

It is further ordered that Respondent be assessed the costs and expenses of these proceedings, with legal interest to commence running thirty days from the date of finality of this ruling until paid.

Rule 1.5. Fees.

* * * *

(e) A division of fee between lawyers who are not in the same firm may be made only if:

(1) The division is in proportion to the services performed by each lawyer or, by written agreement with the client, each lawyer assumes joint responsibilities for the representation;

(2) The client is advised of and does not object to the participation of all the lawyers involved; and

(3) The total fee is reasonable.

[ii] State Bar Articles of Incorporation, Art. 16, Rules of Prof. Conduct, Rule 1.5, LSA-R.S. foll. 37:221

Rule 1.7. Conflict of Interest: General Rule.

Loyalty is an essential element in the lawyer's relationship to a client. Therefore:

(a) A lawyer shall not represent a client if the representation of that client will be directly adverse to another client, unless:

(1) The lawyer reasonably believes the representation will not adversely affect the relationship with the other client; and

(2) Each client consents after consultation.

(b) A lawyer shall not represent a client if the representation of that client may be materially limited by the lawyer's responsibilities to another client or to a third person, or by the lawyer's own interests, unless;

(1) The lawyer reasonably believes the representation will not be adversely affected; and

(2) The client consents after consultation. When representation of multiple clients in a single matter is undertaken, the consultation shall include explanation of the implications of the common representation and the advantages and risks involved.
